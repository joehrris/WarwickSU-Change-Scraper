FAQ
What is TOTUM?
TOTUM is the UK's leading discount platform for students, professionals, apprentices and public sector workers. Offering both free and paid membership options, TOTUM provides access to hundreds of discounts from major brands including Apple, Samsung, ASOS, Boots, Cineworld, Morrisons and many more. Members also benefit from cashback, proof-of-age ID and worldwide discounts.
What's the difference between TOTUM and TOTUM+?
TOTUM is our free digital membership giving you access to hundreds of online discounts. TOTUM+ is our paid membership starting at £14.99/year that unlocks exclusive discounts not available to free members, plus a physical card for in-store use. You'll also get a free TOTUM Cashback card (saving members an average of £243/year on groceries), free government-approved 18+ ID, and access to over 100,000 discounts across 90+ countries. TOTUM+ members save on average £550 per year.
How much does TOTUM+ cost and is it worth it?
TOTUM+ costs just £14.99 for one year (less than £1.25 per month), £24.99 for two years (less than £1.05 per month), or £29.99 for three years (less than 85p per month). Members save on average £550 annually through exclusive discounts, meaning you could recoup your membership cost over 35 times in just one year. The included TOTUM Cashback card alone helps members save an average of £243 per year on supermarket shopping – that's more than 16 times the cost of an annual membership just from groceries. In simple terms: spend from £14.99, save over £790 on average per year.
Who can join TOTUM?
TOTUM is available to students aged 16+ in further or higher education, professionals (including part-time learners and members of chartered institutes), UK apprentices studying Level 2-7 qualifications, and public sector workers with registered government organisation email addresses.
How quickly can I start saving with TOTUM?
Once you've completed sign-up and payment, you can immediately start using digital discounts by downloading the TOTUM app and logging in. Your physical TOTUM+ card will arrive by post shortly after.
Can professionals get TOTUM membership?
Absolutely. TOTUM is available to professionals who are part-time learners studying for professional or accredited qualifications, current members of chartered institutes or professional bodies, academic or support staff at training providers, recent graduates, or employees at government-run public sector organisations.
What are the eligibility requirements for students?
Students must be aged 16+ and enrolled at a sixth form, school, college or university in the UK. Your course must require at least 10 hours of study per week for a minimum of 12 weeks (or 120 hours total) and should be a recognised accredited qualification.
Does TOTUM include proof-of-age ID?
TOTUM+ members can receive a free government-approved 18+ proof-of-age ID card. You'll need your passport or UK driving licence to complete the ID application, which can be done during sign-up or within 21 days of purchasing your membership.
How do I verify my eligibility?
Verification is straightforward and depends on your community. Students typically use their university or college email address. Professionals verify through their organisation email, professional body membership details, or verification codes. Apprentices use their apprenticeship details or training provider information. Public sector workers use their government organisation email address.
What do I need to sign up?
You'll need a debit or credit card, a photo for your membership card, and proof of eligibility. Verification methods include your organisation email address (university, employer, etc.), apprenticeship details, professional body membership, or a verification code from your organisation. For the optional government-approved ID, you'll need your passport or UK driving licence.
Can I use TOTUM internationally?
Yes, TOTUM+ members have access to 100,000s of discounts in over 90 countries worldwide, making your membership valuable wherever you go.
Can public sector workers join TOTUM?
Yes, employees with registered email addresses at government-run public sector organisations are eligible for TOTUM and TOTUM+ memberships.
Are apprentices eligible for TOTUM?
Yes, all UK apprentices studying recognised qualifications are eligible. This includes Intermediate Apprenticeships (Level 2), Advanced Apprenticeships (Level 3), Higher Apprenticeships (Level 4/5) and Degree Apprenticeships (Level 6/7).
