This event is a dedicated day for students who menstruate to access free, expert-led information on menstrual and reproductive health. It aims to create an open, supportive space to learn more about common and lesser-known conditions, better understand symptoms, and feel empowered to seek help when needed.
Alongside the educational sessions, there will be opportunities to ask questions anonymously and access confidential one-to-one support. The day will be led by
Alex from Lunaire
, who will host all sessions and share expert guidance throughout.
Schedule
09:00 – 10:00
Menstrual Health & Navigating Worrying Symptoms
10:30 – 11:30
Endometriosis & Adenomyosis
12:00 – 13:00
Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome (PCOS)
14:00 – 15:00
Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD)
15:30 – 16:30
Anonymous Q&A Forum
17:00 – 18:00
6 x 1 to 1 Confidential Drop-In Support sessions
Locations
Sessions from 09:00 – 15:00 will take place in the Kevin Gately Room
Anonymous Q&A: Green Room (first door on the left in SUHQ Floor 1)
1:1 Drop-In Support: MR4 (Meeting Room 4)
Tickets for each of the 6 x 10 minute 1 to 1 drop-in sessions are £3. This is a deposit to hold your place, and it will be refunded at the SU Welcome Hub after you have attended your session.
Organised by VP Welfare and Campaigns, Ollie Chapman, and Women’s Officer, Katie Todd.
