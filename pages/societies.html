Adam is the elected VP Societies of the Students' Union. He sits on the Societies Exec and is a member of the Board of Trustees.

Warwick SU has over 250 societies covering a wide range of student interests, so there is bound to be one for you! On this page you can find out more about societies and join up, along with information about starting a new society and resources for exec members.

Report + Support

Everyone studying at, working at, and visiting Warwick has the right to feel safe. Report and Support is the University’s disclosure platform for all forms of Harassment including Sexual Misconduct, Discrimination, Bullying and Hate Incidents. Report & Support Ready is an accreditation scheme, which equips the Execs of Sports Clubs and Societies to safely receive disclosures, signpost effectively and promote healthy cultures within their communities. By completing the relevant training to be accredited as ‘Report & Support Ready’, Sports Clubs and Societies can commit to join our mission to tackle all forms of harassment at Warwick.

How much does it cost to join a Society?

To become an official member of a Society, you will need to purchase two things:

  1. A one-off Societies Federation fee
  2. A fee to join the society.

The Societies Federation fee is a one-off annual fee that enables you to join as many Societies as you wish and will vary depending on your student status. Prices below:

  • Undergraduate – £12
  • Postgraduate and Degree Apprentice – £6
  • ERASMUS and Study Abroad - £6

Societies set their own membership fee. Some Societies are free to join, whilst some Societies will charge a small fee to join their Society, which help fund some of their activities for the year.

How do I join Societies?
  1. Search for the Society/Societies you wish to join. The list of Societies is available below
  2. Click “Join This Club”
  3. Once you’ve selected all of the Societies you wish to join, go to your basket.
  4. There it will ask you to join Societies Federation. Select and add the Societies Federation fee which applies to you (Undergrad, Postgrad or ERASMUS)
  5. Pay for your products and you are officially a member of those societies
How do I start a new society?

If none of our 250+ societies float your boat then you can always set one up yourself! You just have to make sure that your society is unique and not doing something a current society already does. Click here to find out more .

What if I’m Staff or an Associate member?

If you are a Staff or an Associate member, please click here to find out more.

How long does my Societies Federation and Society Membership last?

Your membership will be active from the point at which you purchase it, until the 31st August.
