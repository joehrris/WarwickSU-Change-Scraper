Welcome to the Funding Hub!

Here you will find information about our student-led funds which provide necessary financial support to our student community, making everyday life at university that little bit easier!

These funds help to reduce barriers of participation in sport (Sportswear For All), provide essential items to support students with disabilities (Disability Item Fund), and support trans, non-binary, and gender non-conforming students to purchase gender-affirming items (Gender Expression Fund). Click on each fund tab for further details!

Any current University of Warwick student can apply for each fund by filling out an application form below within the application window. Once the fund has closed, the student-led panel will assess applications and contact applicants through their @warwick.ac.uk email addresses with the outcome.

If these funds don't quite match what you're looking for on an individual basis, we also have the 'Fund Your Idea' section, which alllows you to apply for a larger amount of money to test an idea, support a campaign or develop a project further.

Specific societies and sports funding can also be found on the Finance - Funding page .

Please fully read the terms & conditions for each fund before applying!

If you have any questions please contact your SU Coordinator

Sportswear For All

The Sportswear For All Fund will provide financial assistance to support individuals and clubs who want to purchase any sportswear that would benefit their welfare and allow them to feel comfortable participating sport. Items that would be covered by the fund include gender-affirming items, cultural wear, religious wear, disability support and any other items to support and encourage inclusivity and diversity within sport.

Amount

The Sportswear For All Fund of £4,000 can be applied for:

  • Up to £50 for an individual
  • Up to £200 for a sports club

Examples of items

Examples of items that can be applied for include (but not limited to) a swimwear Hijab, chest protectors, earplugs, modified equipment for a physical disability, and more.

Key Dates

  • Applications open 9am Monday 6th October 2025
  • Applications close 12pm Friday 8th May 2026

We are working through applications/orders in order of priority, we will contact everyone as soon as we can. If you were unsuccessful you may not hear back due to cappacity.

Terms & Conditions

Please read the terms & conditions for the Sportswear For All Fund before making your application as this may delay the process or void your application

  • Be a current student at The University of Warwick.

  • Be in need of financial support to access sport participation.

  • Be able to provide links to the item(s) you wish to purchase with the fund that come to a total monetary value of either up to £50 for individuals or up to £200 for a sports club.

  • Be able to fund the purchase initially and receive reimburesment, or collect these item(s) from the Warwick Students’ Union HQ Building. For exact location, see the Warwick Interactive Map.

  • Be using the item(s) to feel comfortable participating in sport. This could be for independent exercise, as a member of a University of Warwick sports club, a member of the Sports & Wellness Hub, or other alternatives.

  • If we do not hear back from you following confirmation of successful application, your application may be cancelled and funding will be passed on to another applicant.

Guidance on Choosing Items

  • This fund is to support individuals and clubs who want to purchase any sportswear or equipment that would benefit their wellbeing and enable them to feel more comfortable participating in sport.
  • Item(s) could include gender-affirming clothing, cultural wear, religious wear, disability support, and other items which support and encourage participation in sport. (See Examples and Retailers below)
  • Your item(s) must be available in an online store with delivery in the UK – we cannot go and collect items in-person.
  • You can list multiple items, as long as you do not exceed £50 in total. However, if you do, please make sure you rank items in order of importance (which ones you’d prefer most to least.)
  • Please make sure you check sizing guides. It may not be possible for exchanges to be made if the clothing doesn’t fit or the item is not eligible for exchange.
  • If the item(s) you want exceeds £50, and the store you are looking to purchase it from sells gift cards, we can part-fund via purchase of a gift card. Please include this in your application if applicable.

Examples & Retailers

  • Female chest protectors for fencing – thefencingshop.co.uk
  • Binders – spectrumoutfitters.co.uk
  • Weather protective niqab / hijab – trekmates.co.uk
  • Full-coverage swim tunic – nike.com
  • Earplugs for loud noises – loopearplugs.com

Data Usage

The data provided for the Sportswear For All application form may be used:

  • To assess applications and award money to applicants
  • To prepare a report at the end of the project that will be shared with stakeholders of the Warwick Innovation Fund and Warwick Students’ Union. No data that allows any individual to be personally identified will be included in this report (including names, student IDs, emails.)

Application

There are 4 sections in the application form:

  • Data Usage Agreement (compulsory) - so that we can process your application for the Sportswear For All Fund
  • Personal Details (compulsory) - so we can contact you and so that we can confirm you’re eligible to apply to the fund. We will use official @warwicksu.com email addresses to communicate with applicants.
  • Item(s) Request (compulsory) - so that we know what we’re ordering for your application. If multiple items are requested, please rank them in order of importance (which ones you’d prefer most to least.)

Disability Item Fund

The Disability Item Fund will provide financial assistance for students to purchase disability items that would help with university and everyday life. This could include items such as stim toys, weighted blanket, books, sensory items, ear defenders, disability pride items, sunflower lanyard, radar key, non-slip matting, and more.

Amount

The Disability Item Fund of £4,000 can be applied for:

  • Up to £50 by an individual (applications exceeding £50 up to £100 may be considered)

Examples of item

Examples of items that can be applied for include (but not limited to) are stim toys, weighted blanket, books, sensory items, ear defenders, disability pride items, sunflower lanyard, radar key, non-slip matting and more.

Key Dates

  • Applications open Nov 2025
  • Applications close Mar 2026 ( End of Week 10)

Terms & Conditions

Please read the terms & conditions for the Disability Item Fund before making your application as this may delay the process or void your application

  • Be a current student at The University of Warwick.
  • Be in need of financial support to purchase disability support item(s).
  • Be able to provide links to the item/service(s) you wish to purchase with the fund that come to a total monetary value (including shipping and any VAT imports to the UK) of up to £100.
  • Be able to come in and purchase these item(s) from the Warwick Students’ Union HQ Building by the end of the 2025-26 academic year if you are shipping your items to the Student Union. For exact location, see the Warwick Interactive Map.

There is NO requirement to evidence a disability or be registered with the Disability Services as we acknowledge that there are substantial barriers to diagnosis.

Guidance on Choosing Items

  • This fund is to support students who want to purchase disability items that would help with university and everyday life.
  • Item(s) could include stim toys, weighted blanket, books, sensory items, ear defenders, disability pride items, sunflower lanyard, radar key, non-slip matting, and more. (See Examples and Retailers below.)
  • Your item(s) must be available in an online store with delivery – we cannot physically go and collect items in-person.
  • You can list multiple items, as long as you do not exceed £50 in total. However, if you do, please make sure you rank items in order of importance (which ones you’d prefer most to least.)
  • Please make sure you check sizing guides. It may not be possible for exchanges to be made if the clothing doesn’t fit or the item is not eligible for exchange.
  • If the item(s) you want is currently out of stock, you can request a gift card if the shop you wish to purchase from sells them. If the item you want goes out of stock between your application and our purchasing, we will contact you to seek an alternative, whether this be a gift card, a different colour, or another item entirely.
  • If the item(s) you want exceeds £50, and the store you are looking to purchase it from sells gift cards, we can part-fund via purchase of a gift card. Please include this in your application if applicable. Please note, there is no other way for us to part-fund for logistical reasons.

Examples & Retailers

  • Stim toys – stimology.co.uk
  • Disability pride items – disabilityhorizons.com
  • Sunflower lanyard - hdsunflower.com
  • Radar key – disabilityrightsuk.org
  • Ear defenders – amazon.co.uk

This is not an exhaustive list of examples / retailers

Data Usage

The data provided for the Disability Item Fund application form will be used:

  • To assess applications and award money to applicants
  • To prepare a report at the end of the project that will be shared with stakeholders of the Warwick Innovation Fund and Warwick Students’ Union. No data that allows any individual to be personally identified will be included in this report (including names, student IDs, emails.)

If you wish to withdraw your application and have your submitted data deleted at any time, please email campaigns@warwicksu.com with the subject line ‘Disability Item Fund – Data Withdrawal’

Amount

The Gender Expression Fund of £4,000 can be applied for:

  • Up to £50 by an individual

Examples of Items

Gender-affirming items, such as (but not limited to): new clothes/underwear, chest binders, breast forms, packers, wigs, affirming sports/swimwear, makeup/nail polish, pronoun badges, and more.

For a list of items and retailers, check out this detailed Guides page

Key Dates

Sports Leadership Fund

The Leadership Fund is a new fund from Warwick Students’ Union (SU) that aims to support the personal and professional development of student sport leaders across the University. The fund offers partial financial support to students pursuing recognised coaching or officiating qualifications, helping to build valuable skills, enhance leadership experience, and make a positive impact within the wider Warwick community.

  • Applications open 27th October 2025
  • Applications close 12pm Friday 8th May 2026

We are working through applications in order of priority; we will contact everyone as soon as we can.

Fund Terms & Eligibility:

  • Be a current student at The University of Warwick.
  • Be able to provide links or evidence of the course/qualification you wish to complete/have completed.
  • Be able to fully fund the course or qualification initially and receive reimbursement.
  • Students are not required to have completed their course or qualification at the time of applying for the fund. However, they must have successfully completed the course or qualification before logging any volunteering hours in order to be eligible for reimbursement. Applicants are also required to declare within their application the anticipated date of course completion.
  • Applications will be reviewed on a first come first served basis until the total fund limit is reached.
  • There is a limit of one qualification per student, per academic year.
  • The total value of supported qualifications per club is capped at £500. Individuals are required to disclose any club membership in their application.
  • If we do not hear back from you following confirmation of successful application, your application may be cancelled, and funding will be passed on to another applicant.
  • Funding is limited to a maximum of 50% of the total course cost, up to £150 per student per academic year.

To receive funding reimbursement, students must complete the following minimum volunteering commitments:

  • Refereeing: 3 fixtures (or equivalent of 4.5 hours)
  • Coaching: 8 hours
  • Other activities: To be determined between staff and student on a case-by-case basis.

Funding will only be reimbursed once:

  • The required number of volunteering hours has been completed via our approved volunteering log sheet.
  • Verification and supporting evidence have been submitted and approved.
  • Hours cannot be backdated or claimed retrospectively if completed before course payment and completion.

Application

There are 5 sections in the application form:

  • Data Usage Agreement
  • Personal Details
  • Course information
  • Personal statement
  • Terms & conditions agreement

FAQ’s:

  • Do I have to be part of a sports club to apply for the fund?

No, you do not need to be part of a sports club to apply for this fund.

  • Can I apply for more than one qualification in a year?

No, each student can only apply for one qualification per academic year.

  • Do I need to complete the qualification before applying?

No, you do not need to have completed your course to apply. However, you must successfully complete the qualification before logging any volunteering hours in order to be eligible for reimbursement. You also need to provide the anticipated date of course completion in your application.

  • Do I need to pay for the course upfront?

Yes, students must initially fund the course themselves and will be reimbursed once all requirements are met.

  • What if my qualification isn’t running until a later date?

You can submit an application for a qualification scheduled for a future date. However, the qualification must include a confirmed date and relevant details. We cannot accept applications for qualifications without confirmed information. Once your application is approved, we will contact you closer to the start date to provide guidance on the fund and voluntary hours requirements.

If you have any questions or queries regarding the Sports Leadership Fund, please contact Gemma Dawson (Sports Coordinator) on gemma.dawson@warwicksu.com.
