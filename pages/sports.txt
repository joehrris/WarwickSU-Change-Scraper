Louis Gosling - VP Sports
Louis is the elected VP Sports of the Students' Union. He chairs the Sports Exec and is a member of the Board of Trustees.
Sports Clubs are entirely student-run and are a key part of the Students’ Union and the opportunities offered to all students at Warwick. Clubs also benefit from support from Warwick Sport; the heart of sport, fitness and an active lifestyle at the University of Warwick. Warwick Sport provides Sports Clubs with state-of-the-art sport and fitness facilities, including the new Sport & Wellness Hub that opened in Spring 2019.
With such a wide variety of sports clubs available, this is a really exciting time to get join. So, what are you waiting for? Get involved and be part of something special!
Report + Support
Everyone studying at, working at, and visiting Warwick has the right to feel safe. Report and Support is the University’s disclosure platform for all forms of Harassment including Sexual Misconduct, Discrimination, Bullying and Hate Incidents. Report & Support Ready is an accreditation scheme, which equips the Execs of Sports Clubs and Societies to safely receive disclosures, signpost effectively and promote healthy cultures within their communities. By completing the relevant training to be accredited as ‘Report & Support Ready’, Sports Clubs and Societies can commit to join our mission to tackle all forms of harassment at Warwick.
Active Bystander Training
Active bystanders intervene to prevent harm to members of their communities. Four members of exec from each club are required to complete a ten-hour course in which they develop their knowledge, skills and confidence to make safe and effective interventions in response to harmful behaviours and attitudes (like sexual harassment, racism, sexism, homophobia and transphobia). This helps to make clubs and societies safe and welcoming at Warwick. You can find out more about how to be an active bystander
by clicking here
.
There are two types of clubs
Off-campus: clubs that require specific training facilities such as Equestrian or Skiing/ Snowboarding which Warwick Sport do not provide.
On-campus: clubs that train in any of Warwick Sport’s facilities, such as Table Tennis, Rugby or Trampolining.
If you are joining more than one club and one is off-campus and the other is on-campus, then you only need to purchase just one on-campus Club Pass.
How much does it cost to join a Sports Club?
To join a Sports Club, it’s essential to purchase a Warwick Sport Club Pass, the Sports Federation membership and the club joining fee in order to join a Sports Club. Read more about fees
by clicking here
.
How do I join Sports Clubs?
Step 1:
You can then join as many clubs as you like! View all Sports clubs below.
Step 2:
Once you’ve decided what clubs you’d like to become a member of, you will need to purchase either the on-campus Warwick Sport Club Pass (£82) or off-campus Warwick Sport Club Pass (£22). The Club Pass will enable you access facilities for training and matches, as well as development opportunities and personalised support from the dedicated Warwick Sport Club Development team.
Step 3:
Last but not least, you will need to become a member of the SU’s Sports Federation. A large proportion of this £18 membership fee goes directly to Sports Clubs, while the rest pays for things like insurance, staff support and admin costs.
How do I upgrade my Off-Campus Club Pass to an On-Campus Club Pass?
If you've decided that you’d like to join a Sports Club which requires an On-Campus Club Pass, but you’ve already purchased an Off-Campus Club Pass, it is easy to upgrade.
Once you have added your new membership to the basket, go to the "Review Basket" page where the Upgrade Club Pass will be available for purchase. Upgrades cost £60
