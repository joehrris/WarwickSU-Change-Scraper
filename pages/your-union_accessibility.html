Warwick SU Accessibility

Accessibility doesn’t just mean wide-door toilets or ramps, and disability doesn’t just apply to people in wheelchairs. The spectrum of disabilities is wide and varied, and many disabilities are hidden (e.g. dyspraxia, dyslexia, Autistic Spectrum Disorders, Attention Deficit Disorders and more).

Every person experiences disability differently. Sometimes people with mental difficulties (eg. anxiety, depression) will identify as disabled – but sometimes they won’t. Similarly, people with long-standing medical conditions may be classed as disabled and be entitled to support.

Our disabled members often experience additional barriers when accessing their education, and this includes SU activities such as eating in our outlets, participating in a club/society, getting involved in democracy or going out in the venues. It is virtually impossible to cater for every kind of access need, but we aim to reduce these barriers wherever we can.

In February 2016, we were awarded Attitude is Everything’s charter at Silver level. We also signed a pledge with Time to Change in February 2011 to take action in order to reduce mental health discrimination.

Students' Union building and SUHQ building layout:

If you would like to view the layout of our building before you enter, the best way to do so is via the Warwick University interactive map found here https://warwick.ac.uk/about/visiting/maps/interactive/ .

On this map you can search for Students Union and it will give you the option of the Students Union (the building housing most of our commercial outlets) or SUHQ (the building housing the Canopy restaurant, our offices and services such as Advice and Welfare).

This page obviously lets you see where the building is situated on campus along with things like the bus station and car parks so you cvan find your way, but if you click on the building you are interested in you can also select floor plans and be able to see where the various outlets are located within the building, where the toilets are, and where stairs/lifts are located.

Accessibility Improvements:

Having our commercial building fully refurbished in 2009, we believed we were fairly compliant with regards to the Disability Discrimination Act (DDA) 1995. However, during an informal chat with Jenny Wheeler (2014/15 SU Disabled Students Officer), it became apparent that there were a number of areas where we could improve.

Below are various ongoing action plans, for our general building and for our various outlets, that outline our current focus for these areas.

If you have any other suggestions or feedback, please get in contact with us.

The various ways of doing so can be found here https://www.warwicksu.com/your-union/contact/

For operational matters and buildings queries you can contact Duncan.Parkes@WarwickSU.com

Local Venues Accessibility Guide

This guide aims to show you Accessibility of local Venues when arranging outings, balls and socials.

Accessibility affects a wide number of students, all experiencing different things that you may not consider when booking a venue.

We’ve been round local venues in Coventry and Leamington Spa, which are regularly used by our Clubs and Societies to audit their accessibility.

It’s not our aim to shame anyone—we just want to make sure students know what different types of access each venue has.
